PROJECT NAME

Alto

DATE

November 2018

MATERIALS

Human Centered Design, Product Design, UI Design, Figma

Details

Alto is a patient to provider digital solution designed to keep aging patients engaged and informed in between their appointments.

Research

1 in 10 therapy patients drop out of their scheduled appointments.


Question: Why are there so many no-shows?


Patient sided interviews revealed that patients didn’t feel engaged with their therapist.


“They didn’t really know me.” was a common phrase in our interviews.


Interviews with providers like Mount Sinai revealed inconsistencies with current patient intake processes.


“It’s a big waste of time. You have to open so many different pages and they take forever to load.” Another common phrase from our provider side interviews.

Synthesis

From our interviews, we are derived two areas of need for both patients and providers.


Patients need to have a personal connection to their therapist; the more information a therapist can cite the better. Patients also need to feel like their relationship continues beyond their scheduled appointments— as topics from their sessions can linger or are left unaddressed.


Providers need to keep drop out rates low by engaging with their patients on a personal level. They also need a quick and efficient method of accessing each patient’s information while respecting privacy.

Solution

We created an engaging and intuitive line of products for both the patients and providers.

One is designed to be a non-intrusive application that assists with a patient's intake process. Which also serves as a catalyst for patient and therapist interaction by providing simple exercises and techniques that help patients feel engaged.



The other product is focused on the intake process. It is designed to display data as efficiently as possible. This high efficiency also ensures snappy load times between pages.

Next Steps

Design and Create solutions that further emphasize our quick and effective methods of engagement while adopting a none intrusive approach. Apple Watch or voice interfaces like the Alexa devices show promise for future development.
