Details

NYU Classes is a digital platform designed to help faculty provide course materials and lessons for students.

Unfortunately, it is often described as:

“Broken, confusing, frustrating, boring, old, and ugly.”

It was clear to me that this needed to change.

Thanks to the collaborative nature of the W.R. Innovation Labs, I was able to meet like-minded individuals who also wanted to see change.

Early development

Shortly after discovering that the site could be customized with CSS, we approached professors who primarily taught online classes and found that there was indeed a demand for customized course site.

We then learned that NYU Stern had been wanting to expand its online education programs. Thus, we focused our efforts on redesigning courses taught mostly online.

Accessibility and Design Components

After a phone interview with a visually impaired student, we realized that our designs had to be usable and accessible to everyone in the community.

Another interview made it absolutely clear that our work needed to be easy to import, edited and used by non-technical staff members.

We realized that redesigning the entirety of the site would be no easy task. We learned that the CMS provider, Sakai, was constantly under development and didn’t allow the use of any JavaScript

 

Despite this obstacle, we found clever, accessible and scalable solutions that allowed us to transform NYU classes into something that is:

“Mobile-first,user centric, and beautiful.”

Becoming a leader

Initially, it was my role to design and develop web solutions without the use of Javascript. This proved to be extremely difficult as each course had varying amounts of interactivity. I would often orchestrate meetings with the creators of Sakai in order to help my team understand their development process and our own constraints.

My role on this project evolved from a developer/designer role to a product manager / technical consultant role; where I co-lead a small team of designers, developers, and education technologists. Growing into a leadership role was extremely humbling and it made me excited to help other learn more about design principals and front end development.
