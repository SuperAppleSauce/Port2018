Details

This dome-shaped building would be a public space for students and citizens alike to relax and explore. Inside the ceiling of the dome, is an interactive projection mapping display. The projection displays realtime videos of skies around the world at that exact time. Because the dome is made of crystal, whatever shows on the inside is also visible to others passing by. On the lower floor, there is public seating, and an interactive floor offers a delight to children and adults alike. As users walk on the lower floor, animations follow their footsteps.

This structure was designed to be implemented in New York City, where the cold winter weather often keeps people from enjoying the sunlight and nature that is usually available in the summer. This piece seeks to offer a calm, naturalistic space where users can enjoy the environment while still being free from the cold. Because of the crystal ceiling, light seeps in to the basement past the glass floor of the first floor, feeding enormous trees that dot the bottom level. Creating a greenhouse-like space enables guests to enjoy the sunlight while meeting friends for a meal, or just simply taking a moment to relax. The ramp that connects the ground floor to the basement is designed to be accessible as well as blend into the design of the entire structure.
